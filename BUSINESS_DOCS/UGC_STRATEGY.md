# Moood! User-Generated Content (UGC) Strategy

**Last Updated**: December 15, 2025  
**Purpose**: Define the strategy for transitioning from manual curation to user-generated content to scale the design library cost-efficiently

**The Challenge**: Manually curating 30,000+ designs is expensive and time-consuming.

**The Solution**: Transform Moood! Collector into a **Personal Design Library** where users save designs for themselves first, then optionally share with the community. This inverts the UGC model from altruism to self-interest, creating network effects that scale the library organically while reducing curation costs by 70-90%.

**The Key Insight**: Users don't upload "for the community"â€”they **save for themselves** (Pinterest replacement). Sharing is optional and incentivized.

**The Timeline**: 
- **Month 0-4**: 100% manual curation (3,000 designs)
- **Month 5-6**: Enable Personal Library (all users can save privately)
- **Month 7-8**: 30-50% share publicly (optional)
- **Month 9+**: 70-90% UGC from personal libraries

**The Impact**: 
- Reduce curation costs from $88/1,000 designs to ~$9/1,000 designs
- Scale library to 30,000+ designs by Year 2
- **Lock-in effect**: Users depend on their personal library (retention)
- **Higher UGC rate**: 30-50% vs 10-20% with traditional model
